On-site Visits

The To Lich River is a small river located in Hanoi, the capital city of Vietnam. It flows through the western part of Hanoi and is one of its many rivers and water bodies. Historically, the To Lich River was once a picturesque and clean waterway, but over the years, it has become heavily polluted due to urbanization and industrial activities in the area. I think this is the most famous polluted river in Vietnam. We've had this problem for several years and still have not yet found a solution.

Insufficient waste collection and treatment infrastructure contribute to the problem. This includes inadequate sewage systems, illegal dumping of garbage directly into the river, and limited capacity for treating industrial waste. Unauthorized construction and encroachment onto the riverbanks have reduced the natural buffer zone, increasing the vulnerability of the river to pollution.

Efforts have been made to clean and restore the To Lich River to its former state. In recent years, the government and various organizations in Hanoi have been implementing environmental projects to improve water quality and rejuvenate the river. These efforts include measures like treating wastewater, planting trees along the riverbanks, and raising public awareness about the importance of keeping the river clean.

While these efforts are encouraging, the To Lich River remains heavily polluted. Cleaning it up will require sustained commitment from the government, businesses, and individuals to address all the contributing factors.
