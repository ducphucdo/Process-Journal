Postproduction and Colour

Now it’s time to bring these footages to life by giving them a more vibrant colours and more saturation.
Colours can be used to guide the audience's attention and reinforce the narrative. Filmmakers may use contrasting colours to draw attention to specific elements or desaturate colours during reflective or sombre moments to enhance the gravity of the subject matter.
  • By fine-tuning colours, we can achieve a polished and professional appearance, elevating the visual quality of the production and making it more engaging for the audience
  • Here is my node tree in Davinci Resolve, from left to right my node are ”White Balance”, “PreLog”, “Log” and to parallel nodes are “Post Log”

    • White Balance: Adjust the temperature of the image (can be lean toward cold or warm)

    PreLog: Correcting the exposure before applying “Colour Space Transform” into the “Log” node

    Log: Contain ”Colour Space Transform” (S-log3. Gammut converted to Rec.709)

    PostLog: Further adjustment with a more advanced colour grade, in this case, I added green for the leaves and a bit more saturation.
