Mind Map of 3 Themes

Motion in Fine Arts

Motion in fine art refers to the illusion or depiction of movement or dynamism within a static artwork. The representation of a sense of action, change, or progression engages the viewer and imparts a feeling of energy, flow, or life to the piece. Artists achieve this effect through various techniques, including composition, brushstrokes, colour, perspective, and other design elements.
In traditional visual arts, artists have explored motion by using blurring, repetition, overlapping forms, and dynamic compositions to suggest movement. For instance, a painter might use brushstrokes that evoke a sense of speed or use positioning and angles to create the illusion of a figure in motion.
With the advent of technology in contemporary art, motion has expanded to include digital media, animation, video art, and interactive installations. Artists can now incorporate movement, energy, and dynamic elements into their work, often engaging the audience in a more immersive experience.

Why this theme: The theme allows me to blend the beauty of traditional art, particularly brushstrokes reminiscent of famous painters, with the dynamic, moving canvas of film.

Visual Starting point (Motion in Fine Arts)

  • Experimentation in combining fine arts, motion graphics, and VFX to create a unique visual narrative
  • Explore the integration of fine arts, motion graphics, and VFX in a short film that emulates the brushstrokes
  • Use photography to capture fleeting moments and emotions, then transform them into a short film with motion graphics and VFX

Water Pollution and Restoration

This is the theme that focused into the critical issue of water pollution and the restoration of rivers and water bodies. It encompasses various aspects related to rivers, their significance, the challenges they face due to pollution, and the efforts to restore and conserve them
Given my interest in photography, art direction, film, and short film, this theme provides a perfect canvas for my artistic expression. It allows me to use my creativity to tell a compelling visual story encouraging action and raising awareness.
The idea is to present a detailed overview of the different types of water pollution, including industrial runoff, agricultural runoff, plastic waste, chemical pollutants, and their impacts on aquatic life and ecosystems.

Why this theme: This theme offers an opportunity for a lasting and meaningful environmental impact. By addressing the critical issue of water pollution and conservation, my project can contribute to a more significant movement for sustainable practices and environmental stewardship.

Visual Starting point (Water Pollution and Restoration)

  • A visual narrative that illustrates the challenges of water pollution and efforts to restore rivers and water bodies, emphasizing the importance of clean water for all
  • Incorporate artistic elements like photography, videography, and art installations to visually convey the message of the project and evoke emotions related to the issue
  • Explore specific rivers or water bodies that have been severely impacted by pollution

Digital Dystopia

This theme will be animated short film that focus into a dystopian future influenced by technology, highlighting its potential negative impacts on society, privacy, and human connections.
Delves into the potentially dark and undesirable outcomes of our increasing reliance on technology and its pervasive integration into our lives. It envisions a future where technological advancements if misused or left unchecked, could lead to a society marked by oppression, loss of privacy, and diminished human connection.
Showcase how technology, initially intended to make life easier, becomes a tool of oppression and control. AI-driven surveillance thought policing, or a dystopian internet where information is censored, can be elements illustrating this oppression.

Why this theme: I choose this theme because of my desire for artistic expression and creativity. Crafting a dystopian world through animation, film, or motion graphics allows myself to visually and narratively explore the boundaries of imagination and creativity.

Visual Starting point (Water Pollution and Restoration)

  • Explore the potential dark sides of technological advancement.
  • Creating narratives set in a dystopian future
